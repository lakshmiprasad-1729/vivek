About Disease:

Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS) is a rare neurological disorder where the immune system mistakenly attacks nerves, causing muscle weakness and sometimes paralysis. It usually starts with tingling sensations and can worsen rapidly, affecting breathing and other functions. The cause is often linked to infections. Treatment involves supportive care like therapy or plasma exchange to ease symptoms. Recovery varies, with many improving over time, though it may take a while and require rehabilitation.
